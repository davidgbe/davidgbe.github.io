I am fascinated by a host of topics, from physics, mathematics, neuroscience, machine learning, and programming to music, swimming, and literature. Like many researchers, I have the ultimate goal of understanding the brain and creating intelligence on a non-biological substrate.

Restricted Boltzmann Machines at the Intersection of Physics, Statistics, and Information Theory

There are many parallels between statistical physics and machine learning, but it surprised me quite a bit to see the formalism I'd studied in stat mech turn up in pre-training methods for neural networks. Over the winter of my senior year, I spent time exploring the mechanics of RBMs.

First Principles Molecular Dynamics Assisted by Machine Learning

With Lucas Johnson from USC, I attempted to reproduce the machine learning-aided molecular dynamics described by Zhenwei, et al. A Guassian Process was used to directly infer inter-atomic forces from rotationally and translationally invariant representations of arrangements of atoms.
